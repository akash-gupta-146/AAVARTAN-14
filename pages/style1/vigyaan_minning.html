Vigyaan

Mining

Problem Statements

1. Acid mine drainage

At metal mines, the target ore (like gold, silver, copper, etc.) is often rich in sulphide minerals. When the mining process exposes the sulphides to water and air, together they react to form sulphuric acid. This acid can dissolve other harmful metals and metalloids (like arsenic) from the surrounding rock. Problem: Acid mine drainage is one of mining's most serious threats to water. A mine draining acid can devastate rivers, streams, and aquatic life for hundreds. Suggest and prepare a plan for restricting the discharge of acid water in the water bodies without polluting the environment.

2. Modernization of face machineries in India

In India, use of old variants of face machineries is still undergoing. Use of modern machinery in India is still going at a slow rate compared to the foreign countries like Australia, U.S.A. and China. Problem: Suggest and design a working model of face machineries which are used in other countries but not in India.

3. Improvements in high wall mining in India

High wall mining is a remotely controlled mining method which extracts coal from the face of a coal seam under a high wall in a surface mine, which has reached the final high wall due to uneconomic stripping ratios or due to local constraints which limit further surface mining. SCCL is the 1st company implementing this technology in India. Problem: High-wall mining is a remotely controlled mining method which extracts coal from the face of a coal seam under a high-wall in a surface mine, which has reached the final high-wall due to uneconomic stripping ratios or due to local constraints which limit further surface mining. SCCL is the 1st company implementing this technology in India.

4. Emergency alteration in ventilation

Ventilation is one of the most prominent aspect of an underground mine. Working conditions can be achieved by efficient ventilation. There are situations in which sudden changes are required in ventilation path within fraction of time to ensure safety of mine. Problem: Suggest a technology to divert the ventilating path within minimum possible time and with minimum effort.

5. Mine plan for deposits lying within the buffer zone of Wildlife Sanctuary

Parsa coal block of Adani Ltd. is lying within the elephant corridor of Sarguja, Chattisgarh. Many such valuable deposits are trapped within the areas or the buffer zone of many wildlife sanctuaries in India, Problem: Mining activity is not easily permitted in these buffer zones as it affects precious flora and fauna present in that area. Design a mine plan for such areas which causes minimum or no damage to the flora and fauna as per the regulatory requirement. Parameters: 1. Ground Vibration. 2. Air and water pollution. 3. Forest covers removal. 4. Obstruction in transition corridors.
