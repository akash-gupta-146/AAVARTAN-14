Go Green

Problem Statements

1. You are required to develop an innovative and sustainable design for rooftop toilets which do not use a soak pit/septic tank.

2.Many slums in Raipur and other cities are on illegal lands. Government has neither constructed sewage systems nor will they do it in such locations. Moreover, no community toilets have been provided. The houses are very small, and the lanes very narrow. Some people have managed to construct pucca houses, but they do not have toilets. There is no space to construct soak pits, and narrow lanes do not allow vehicle to come and suck the sludge from the septic tank.
3.Air pollution has been one of the major problems in India, and is mainly caused due to the use of bad fuels and vehicular emissions. Direct emissions into the atmosphere from tailpipes of cars and trucks are the primary sources of vehicular pollution which consists of ozone gas, particulate matter, nitrogen oxides and other hazardous pollutants. Over population and thus increasing personal transport vehicles are major reasons for air pollution in urban areas.
4. Suggest modifications in the existing two-wheeler vehicle design of 100cc engine so that there are zero/less emissions or the emission becomes less harmful for our health. The amount of CO2, CO, NOx and other hazardous pollutants emitted by the improved design should be less than that emitted by existing vehicle.
5. Solar energy is the cleanest�and most abundant renewable energy�source available. Since the sun�s position in the sky changes with the seasons and the time of day, a fixed type solar panel doesn�t receive maximum radiation throughout the day. To receive the maximum intensity of light, we need an automatic solar tracking system so that it aligns according to the sun�s direction thereby receiving sunlight throughout the day irrespective of the sun�s position." />
6. Design an automatic solar tracking system to power a street light with an automatic ON/OFF system so that the street light starts lighting during the night and gets OFF during the day. " />
7. Many rural areas in India lack clean drinking water. In these areas, contaminated water has a significant impact on numerous lives. Fortunately, efficient and cheap water purification systems can make easy access to clean water a reality.
8. Develop a water filtration system for rural areas which comprises of low maintenance, cost-effectiveness, and accessibility of resources.
9.One-third of all the world�s food is wasted every year at enormous economic and environmental cost, finds a United Nations report. The production of this uneaten food is responsible for adding 3.3 billion tonnes of greenhouse gases to the atmosphere. There is a need for a change at every link of the human food chain to prevent food wastage from happening in the first place, and re-use or recycle it when we can�t.
10. Design a working model for producing useful energy from food waste.
11. Cement is used as a building material. Some of the most important environmental problems due to cement production are; greenhouse gas emissions and atmospheric releases, primarily of NOx, SO2, hydrocarbons and particulates and health and safety issues relating to workers and community exposure to dust. Almost 1 tonne of CO2 is released for every 1 tonne of cement produced or 5% of all global greenhouse emissions
12. Propose an innovative ,eco-friendly alternative to cement and compare its properties and characteristics like strength, durability etc. with existing cement
13. Due to the non- corrosive nature of aluminium, its demand is increasing rapidly...Aluminium cans and aerosols can be recycled to help save natural resources. Unfortunately most of the used aluminium cans end up in landfill. When they are discarded to landfill, they can take up 500 years to decompose.
14. Propose mechanisms for converting scrap aluminum into useful products.
15. All the water used up is drowned into low side areas or into forests or into outskirts of cities which cause severe attacks to the people (i.e...causing dengue cholera and typoid ).
16. Propose a Plan and prepare a view of the plan with the help of ecofriendly things.
17. Now-a-days people have learnt to be dependable on almost everything, irrespective of their prospects. So it could really initiate a change if a group of people collectively set up a plan to be more self dependable rather than dependable.

Rules

A team can choose only one problem statement and in case a team opts for technical domain, they have to choose it of their department only.
Team size can be maximum of 3 members.
All participating teams have to prepare an abstract describing the solution and submit it in IEEE format as a PDF file as given in sample abstract by 21/09/2014, 11PM at abstract@aavartan.in .
Along with abstract PDF, you also have to submit another PDF file with the Team details( Name, College, Branch, Sem, Contact, Email ID) of all the team members.
Late Submission won't be entertained.
